Dr. Robert Andrews Millikan

A man who shaped modern physics

Dr. Millikan received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1923.

Here's a brief timeline of Dr. Millikan's life:

  • 1868 - born in Morrison, Illinois.
  • 1893 - receives bachelor's degree in the classics from Oberlin College.
  • 1895 - earns the first doctorate in physics from Columbia University
  • 1902 - marries Greta Ervin Blanchard.
  • 1908 - begins the famous Oil Drop Experiment, which revolutionized particle physics by revealing the mass-to-charge ratio of the electron.
  • 1921 - becomes director of the California Institue of Technology (Caltech).
  • 1923 - wins the Nobel Prize in Physics for his work with on the oil drop experiment.
  • 1945 - leaves position as chairman of the executive council at Caltech.
  • 1953 - dies of a heart attack at his home in San Marino, California.
"I doubt if I have ever taught better in my life than in my first course in physics in 1889. I was so intensely interested in keeping my knowledge ahead of that of the class that they may have caught some of my own interest and enthusiasm."
-Robert A. Millikan
